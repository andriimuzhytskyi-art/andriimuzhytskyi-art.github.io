[ Artist & Experience ] Value Tracker

This product is my own creation. It grew out of my practice as an artist and designer.

Seeing the Full Picture

stacks

[ Artist & Experience ] Value Tracker is a system that shows what the overall value of an artist and their creative journey is truly built on.

Usually, a viewer or collector sees only the finished artwork and its price. This tracker reveals more:

  • how much labor was invested;
  • how much time the process took;
  • what path the artist followed from idea to final result;
  • how the artist’s overall price range and experience are formed.

Simply put, it is a transparent way to show not only the result, but the entire process of creating art and developing as an artist.

Building Trust

shield_with_heart

The tracker helps the artist to:

  • explain to viewers and collectors why the works fall within a specific price range;
  • show that the value of art is not only visual appeal, but also a long and complex process;
  • build trust through openness, where each stage is documented and understandable.

This represents a shift from “simply selling individual artworks” to a conscious dialogue between the artist and the viewer.

The Structure of Value

stacked_line_chart

The tracker consists of two key indicators:

  • Artist Value
  • Experience Value

Together, they create a holistic view of the artist — their value and accumulated experience.

Artist Value

diamond

Artist Value is an indicator of the artist’s cumulative artistic and price value, formed by all works offered for sale (€).

This metric is cumulative — with each new artwork listed for sale, the overall Artist Value increases. It is formed not only by market pricing or subjective valuation, but also by factors that reflect the artist’s level and development:

  • Conceptual depth — the complexity and meaning of ideas within the works;
  • Time and effort — the amount of labor invested in creating the works;
  • Technique — oil painting, digital art, NFTs, or their combination;
  • Style — minimalism, symbolism, surrealism, realism;
  • Volume of work — individual pieces or series;
  • Stage of artistic development — professional and creative growth.

All these parameters together form a clear logic behind the artist’s overall value and price range.

Simply put: Artist Value is the total value of all artworks offered for sale, expressed through a transparent and logical system.

Artist Value is a fixed indicator.
Once an artwork enters the system with an assigned value, it remains unchanged regardless of market conditions, currency fluctuations, or external economic factors.

NFT artworks are fully included in Artist Value through their fixed base artistic value expressed in €. Cryptocurrency is treated solely as a payment medium at the moment of sale and does not define or alter the artistic value of the work within the system.

Experience Value

library_books

Experience Value is a log and an indicator of an artist’s accumulated creative experience (XP). It records all actions taken by the artist during the creation and promotion of artworks and shows that the value of art is built through many consecutive steps.

The journal includes:

  • Preparation — planning, purchasing, and preparing materials;
  • Process — sketches, studies, working stages, intermediate states;
  • Presentation — publications, art and NFT platforms, art fairs, and exhibitions.

Each action is evaluated using an effort-based scale.

Levels and Actions

  • 1 point — Basic action
    Planning, reflection, preliminary ideas.
    Minimal value, as it does not directly lead to the creation of an artwork.
  • 2 points — Informational action
    Publications on social media and online platforms: images, videos, process documentation.
    An important but auxiliary process — communicational rather than artistic.
  • 2 points — Preparatory stages
    Purchasing and preparing materials, technical preparation.
    Concrete actions that create the conditions for artistic work.
  • 3 points — Artistic process
    Sketches, studies, tests, exploration of form and idea.
    Points are assigned for a set of actions or for one day of active work.
  • 3 points — Artistic research
    Visiting museums and galleries; studying works by Old Masters and contemporary artists; visual analysis and comparative observation; working with primary sources; development of artistic language and visual literacy.
    Points are assigned for a set of actions or for one day of work.
  • 3 points — Preparation for presentation
    Preparation of images, texts, and supporting materials.
    Assigned per day of active work.
  • 3 points — Preparation for an exhibition or art fair
    Documentation, logistics, communication.
    Assigned per day of active preparation.
  • 5 points — Significant creation stage
    Work on an artwork: painting, digital art, NFTs.
    Each completed stage is evaluated, not time spent.
  • 10 points — Artwork completion
    Creation of a finished unit of art and its documentation.
  • 50 points — Artwork presentation
    Online or offline presentation of artworks.
    A qualitative transition from a private process to public space.
  • 100 points — Participation in an exhibition or art fair
    An institutional event involving selection, responsibility, and professional recognition.

All points are summed to reflect the real volume of labor, time, and experience accumulated by the artist. The scale is under development and will be expanded and refined as the system evolves.

The Artist as a System

graph_5

The tracker combines the process log and the roadmap into a single system:

  • the roadmap reflects the artist’s development and the growth of their overall value;
  • the process log captures the real volume of labor and accumulated experience.

As a result, [ Artist & Experience ] Value Tracker shows not an individual artwork, but the artist as a complete system — their price range, experience, and developmental path, presented in an honest, clear, and structured way.
